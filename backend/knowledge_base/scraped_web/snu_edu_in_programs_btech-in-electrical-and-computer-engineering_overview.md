B.Tech. in Electrical and Computer Engineering
Overview
The B.Tech. in Electrical and Computer Engineering (ECE) program offered by the Department of Electrical Engineering at the School of Engineering is a comprehensive, future-oriented undergraduate program designed to develop strong foundations in domains such as electrical, electronics, and computing. The curriculum integrates core common curriculum, university wide electives, experiential learning, major core and elective courses, along with a two-phase major project. The program emphasizes hands-on learning, interdisciplinary systems, critical thinking, and exposure to emerging technologies. Students gain in-depth knowledge through departmental specializations in Modern Energy Systems, Wireless Systems Engineering, or VLSI Design. Further, students can also opt for interdisciplinary tracks such as Applied Machine Intelligence, E-Mobility, Cognitive Robotics, and Real-Time Embedded Systems. Each specialization requires completion of a defined set of elective credits, ensuring depth in the chosen domain. The curriculum is structured to foster innovation, practical problem-solving skills, and research aptitude, preparing graduates for intellectually challenging careers in core industries, emerging technology sectors, higher studies, and entrepreneurship.
